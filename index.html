Digital Rights Manifesto

Purpose Statement

The ethical principles of human rights describe the societal function of rights we share simply by virtue of being a human. The concept has been explored since the Middle Ages as an idea of natural rights existing through a theological lens, to the more secularized understanding arising in the 18th century wherein human rights are formed through human reason. Over time and with the evolution and integration of societies, what these rights are remains dynamic; they exist as a response to the conditions that people live in. With new technologies, rights that once helped to define a society’s structure may no longer be pertinent or complete in ensuring humanity can thrive within its current context. With the advent of the internet, and all the consequences - both positive and negative - that arise therein, these universal rights must be adapted to account for such ubiquitous change.

It is not enough for the technology to merely exist when control lies within centralized-autocratic state and corporate powers. The existence of such technology must be moderated to serve humanity as a whole. Agents acting as public benefactors must advocate for:

  • Digital Rights

  • Increased accessibility to the internet for regions and individuals outside of the scope of the internet.
  • Progression in establishing the internet’s infrastructure as a universal public utility.
  • Legislation which serves users before corporations
  • Equal treatment of traffic across the internet.
  • Transparency in the collection and use of user generated data and its metadata.

Goals

  1. Access to the internet represents access to information, education, and increased economic liberation within market economies. The benefits for individuals to become connected have grown so vast that those without access will be unable to compete with those with access. While the general structure of society remains competitive rather than collaborative, universal access to this utility is necessary in providing at least some degree of economic equity.
  2. Municipalities must take on the responsibility of creating internet infrastructures that reach all those within their community. Individuals that live outside of a municipality should have internet access provided by the smallest governing body they reside within. A federated internet infrastructure ultimately provides the most control for individuals.
  3. In the instance that a region’s internet is served through private corporations, efforts must be made to prevent monopolization. Driven by profit, these companies have no reason to improve network accessibility, expanse, or efficiency when there is no competition within the market for users to choose.
  4. Any governing body that manages internet infrastructure must allow equal flow of traffic between entities. Distributing access asymmetrically artificially guides internet traffic to points that are curated by the governing body.
  5. Data generated by users when operating internet-based applications should be owned by those users. All forms of collection, interpretation, and dissemination of this data should be clearly stated to users who give those rights of data ownership over to the entities managing such applications. What data is being collected, how it is being used, and who/what has access to this data must be clearly presented to the user directly through the application.

Closing Statement

The internet is a special technology in that it provides power to individuals while also connecting those individuals together. As a pure tool, it creates opportunity to connect to places previously inaccessible, collaborate with people on a global scale, and create new technologies what were otherwise impossible. Ensuring that the components of this tool remain under the ward of the people is essential in the development of a global, collaborative society. Dissolving centralized control of the internet into the smallest possible pieces is necessary in making the most of this technology, and solidifying the rights of every individual to access information across the globe.
